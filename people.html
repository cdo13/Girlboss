Introducing some Great minds

These 3 brains are some of the black women in college that are dedicated in the encouragement of Black voices, Black Apparel, Black businesses, and Black brains.
All young and growing.

  • Danielle Obisie-Orlu:

  • Danielle is a rising senior who has accomplished so much in her years. She is an immigrant and coins herself as a "third culture kid" becuase she comes from 3 different cultures and backgrounds. She was born from Nigerian parents, Raised in South Africa, and also lived in the United States. Needless to say Danielle is a very cultured and interesting person.
     Her anecdote: Danielle Obisie-Orlu is a Nigerian-American student, actress and poet who grew up in Johannesburg, South Africa. She is a trained public speaker, receiving accolades in speech, drama, and debate. She attends the University of Pittsburgh and is double majoring in International & Area Studies and Political Science, with minors in Sociology and French. She is devoted to fostering environments of intersectional community, belonging, and empowerment. She is passionate about migration, belonging, and international human rights law; partners with ARYSE to empower migrant youth and refugee voices; and is a student leader on her campus. Danielle is the National Youth Poet Laureate Network’s Northeast Regional Runner Up 2022.

Link to Danielle

  • Chelsea Adinuba:

  • Chelsea is a rising sophomore studying business and healthcare administration at the University of Pittsburgh. She started writing her books at the age of 16 and selling her clothing designs and building her brand at the little age of 15! She is also a Pisces.
     Her Personal Anecdote: I started sewing at the age of 14; I had what you could say a "passion for a fashion." I decided to put the gift that God has given me to use and hand sew everything with love. All I had was my gift and faith, and that's what I stepped out with. Matthew 5:14- "You are the light of the World. A city that is set on a hill cannot be hidden." (NKJV). I did not want to hide my gift so I took the little I had and let God make his promise known. Often times we can forget the words that God has once spoken over our lives. As people wear my products they will not only be able remember these words, but also encourage others & bring more people to Christ. I also take old clothes and transform them. I do this because that is what the Holy Spirit does to us. God doesn't just go out and create a new human being, he takes us in our worst state, cleanses us, and saves our soul.

Link to Chelsea

  • AjahDae Edmunds:

  • Ajahdae is a young black journalist who schools at the University of Houston and is so passionate about advertising and empowering black owned businesses. Her website SunnyDae is the definition of black girl magic! She created an easy way to see some black owned brands and kept a very chill aesthetic to make SunnyDae a safe place for everyone to feel comfortable and truly welcome. She is a Gemini.

     Her Anecdote: I am a lifestyle blogger that focuses on different aspects of life. Besides inspiration posts, I have also been doing a small business series that shouts out small businesses, especially black-owned and minority businesses. I am a Gemini sun, Libra rising and Cancer moon! Also, I have always loved to write! I wanted a platform to be able to share my writing to the world and have a place to write
